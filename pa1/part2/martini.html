The Martini

The Old Fashioned

Ingredients Required

  • Vodka or Gin
  • Dry Vermouth
  • Lemon
  • Olive

Instructions

  1. Put 3 ounces of spirit and 1 ounce of vermouth in mixing glass
  2. Stir with ice for 45-60 seconds until glass begins to frost
  3. Strain into martini glass
  4. Garnish with olive and/or lemon peel

By 1922 the martini reached its most recognizable form in which London dry gin and dry vermouth are combined at a ratio of 2:1, stirred in a mixing glass with ice cubes, with the optional addition of orange or aromatic bitters, then strained into a chilled cocktail glass.A dry martini is made with little to no vermouth. Ordering a martini "extra dry" will result in even less or no vermouth added. The exact origin of the martini is unclear. The name may derive from the Martini brand of vermouth. Another popular theory suggests it evolved from a cocktail called the Martinez served sometime in the early 1860s at the Occidental Hotel in San Francisco, which people frequented before taking an evening ferry to the nearby town of Martinez, California.

Variations

Modification Result
Use half vermouth or even less "Dry or Extra Dry Martini"
Use a mix of both gin/vodka and lillet blanc instead of vermouth "Vesper "
